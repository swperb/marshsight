# MarshSight Privacy Policy

_Last updated: June 2026_

MarshSight is built to respect your privacy and your spots. This policy explains
what the app accesses and what, if anything, leaves your device.

## What the app uses

- **Location (GPS and compass).** Used on your device to place navigation
  markers, boundaries, and hazards in the augmented-reality view and to show
  your position on the map. Your location is **not** transmitted or stored on
  any server unless you choose to submit a contribution (see below).
- **Camera.** Used only to display the live augmented-reality view. Camera
  frames are **not** recorded, saved, or transmitted.
- **Motion sensors.** Used on device to stabilize the AR overlay.

## What you can choose to share

- **Contributions** (waypoints, hazards, blinds, ramps, harvest/catch notes).
  When you create one, you choose its visibility:
  - **Private** stays on your device (and your account when accounts exist) and
    is never shown to others. This is the default.
  - **My Group** is shared only with people you choose.
  - **Public** is shared with the community to improve the map for everyone.
  Public and group contributions include the location and details you provide.
  Do not share locations you want to keep secret.
- **Waitlist email.** If you sign up on the website, we store your email only to
  notify you about the beta. We do not sell it or share it.

## What we do not do

- No advertising or third-party ad tracking.
- No selling of personal data.
- No background location tracking. Location is used only while you are using the
  app for navigation.

## Data sources

Map layers come from public-domain government sources (USGS, NOAA, PAD-US). The
app uses no onX data.

## Your control

You can delete the app to remove all local data, including private
contributions stored on your device. To request deletion of public
contributions or your waitlist email, contact us.
